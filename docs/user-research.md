# User Research

## Background

While exploring agriculture-focused product ideas, I wanted to understand the challenges that farmers face during daily decision-making.

Many agricultural decisions depend on weather conditions, experience, and information gathered from multiple sources.

The goal of this research was to identify recurring problems that could be addressed through a digital product.

---

# Research Approach

Research was conducted through:

* Agricultural reports
* Government resources
* Farming communities
* Existing agriculture technology products
* Informal discussions with individuals familiar with farming practices

---

# Target User

## Gurpreet Singh

Age: 45

Occupation: Farmer

Farm Size: 3 Acres

Location: Punjab

### Goals

* Improve crop yield
* Reduce irrigation waste
* Improve planning
* Access timely recommendations

### Pain Points

* Weather uncertainty
* Limited expert guidance
* Manual planning
* Information fragmentation

---

# Key Findings

## Finding 1

Farmers prefer familiar communication channels.

Most users already interact with WhatsApp regularly.

---

## Finding 2

Timeliness matters.

Recommendations lose value when delivered late.

---

## Finding 3

Irrigation planning is difficult.

Farmers often rely on intuition rather than structured recommendations.

---

## Finding 4

Localized recommendations create higher value.

Generic recommendations are often ignored.

---

# Key Insight

The biggest opportunity was not creating more features.

The opportunity was delivering useful recommendations through a channel farmers already trust and use regularly.

---

# Research Outcome

Research helped identify:

* Advisory accessibility
* Irrigation scheduling
* Weather-aware recommendations

as the highest-priority problem areas for the MVP.
